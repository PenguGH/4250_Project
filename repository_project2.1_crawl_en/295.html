Career Outcomes

GIS Analyst/Specialist

Manage and analyze geographic information systems (GIS) to create maps, analyze spatial data, and solve complex geographical problems. Collect and manage spatial data, create digital maps, perform spatial analysis, maintain GIS databases, and provide technical support for projects using specialized software like ArcGIS.

Remote Sensing Specialist

Analyze and interpret data collected from satellites, aircraft, unmanned aerial systems and other remote sensors to study Earth's surface features, natural resources and environmental conditions. Process and analyze satellite imagery, conduct image classification, develop algorithms for data extraction, create detailed maps, monitor environmental changes and integrate remote sensing data with other spatial information systems to support decision-making in fields like agriculture, forestry, urban planning and environmental monitoring.

Emergency Management Planner

Develop and coordinate comprehensive plans to prepare for, respond to and recover from disasters, public health emergencies and other crisis situations to protect communities and minimize impact. Create emergency response protocols, conduct risk assessments, coordinate with multiple agencies, organize emergency drills, manage resources and ensure compliance with regulations while continuously updating plans based on lessons learned from actual emergencies.

Courses

The Geospatial Analysis option prepares you for careers in the dynamic field of spatial data systems. This track focuses on technical skills essential for managing and interpreting geographic data, including satellite and unmanned aerial systems (UAS) imagery and Geographic Information Systems (GIS). You will develop expertise in GIS software and techniques to address issues related to land use, environmental protection and urban planning. Careers in this field often involve working with local governments, environmental agencies, and private sector firms that rely on spatial data for decision-making and policy development. The program also offers a solid foundation for further graduate study in geospatial technology or related areas.
